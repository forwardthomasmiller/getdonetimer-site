Queue vs Delay

The sidebar shows all task statuses

Filter tasks by clicking any category.

What's the difference?

Queued = bumped from the menu bar
The menu bar shows only one task. When a new task starts (manually, scheduled, or from any source), it takes over — the previous task gets pushed into the queue, waiting for its turn back.

Delayed = you chose to set it aside
You manually put the task on hold. It won't auto-start — pick it up whenever you're ready.

Queue & Auto-Run

Control auto-start behavior in Settings

Auto-Start from Queue — when the menu bar task finishes, automatically pull the next queued task back and continue.
Auto-Continue Group Execution — tasks in a group run one after another without manual intervention.

AI Assistant

Bring your own API key

Open AI Assistant tab → Configure API → pick a provider → paste your key → Test Connection. Your key is stored in macOS Keychain and never sent to our servers.

Two ways to create tasks with AI

Create New Tasks — describe what you want, AI designs a complete task plan with time allocation.

Privacy Mode — chat with any external AI (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini...), export JSON, and import locally. Zero data transmission — 100% local processing.

My Notes — AI tips & personal journal

When AI creates a task, it writes key steps and tips into My Notes as a mini cheat-sheet. But My Notes is more than that — it's also your input to AI.

Write down your progress, breakthroughs, or struggles while working. When you need help, describe your situation in My Notes first, then tap Insights — the more context you provide, the better AI's advice will be.

Reuse previous AI plans

Open Conversation History to browse your past AI-generated plans. Click Reuse This Plan to quickly recreate tasks without starting from scratch.
